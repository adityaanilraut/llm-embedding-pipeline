Ray is a distributed-execution framework that lets Python code scale from a
laptop to a cluster with minimal changes. A function decorated with @ray.remote
becomes a task that can be dispatched to any worker in the cluster, and ray
actors give you long-lived stateful workers.

Airflow is a workflow orchestrator. Pipelines are expressed as DAGs of tasks
in Python. Airflow schedules tasks, manages dependencies, retries on failure,
and provides a UI for inspecting historical runs.

In a typical embedding pipeline, Airflow orchestrates the high-level stages
(discover, parse, embed, index) while Ray handles the data-parallel inner
loop of embedding thousands of chunks across many CPU or GPU workers. The
two are complementary: Airflow for control flow, Ray for compute.
